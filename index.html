    About Me

    I build engaged audiences from scratch and turn that engagement into business results.

    That's not just a tagline—it's what I've actually done. I took Tamiltoddlers from zero followers to 5,000+ across YouTube and Instagram, with zero advertising budget. Just consistent, strategic content that solved real problems for Tamil-speaking parents. Along the way, I converted 100+ of those followers into paying customers and scaled internationally to the USA, Singapore, and Norway.

    Now I'm at Google as a Digital Marketing Apprentice, where I've generated 300+ qualified leads worth $3.2M+, closed my first $15K deal, and created content that positions our team as thought leaders. I've learned that great marketing isn't about tricks or hacks—it's about understanding what people need and showing up consistently with value.

    What makes me different? I'm not a traditional marketer. My Computer Science background taught me how to think systematically and break down complex problems. I have an MBA, so I think strategically about business impact, not just marketing metrics. And I spent years teaching 33 kindergarteners simultaneously—if I can keep their attention, I can create content that converts.

    My background is unconventional: engineer → MBA → teacher → entrepreneur → Google. But that's exactly why I bring a fresh perspective. I combine analytical thinking with creative storytelling. I understand both data and human psychology. I love to write copy that drives revenue.

    What I'm looking for: Customer-facing roles in marketing, sales, or customer success where I can leverage my proven ability to build relationships, create compelling narratives, and drive measurable revenue growth. Whether it's product marketing, content marketing, account management, or consultative sales—I thrive in environments where I can connect with customers and help them succeed.

    I'm bilingual (Tamil/English), data-driven, and results-obsessed. But more importantly, I genuinely care about creating marketing that helps people rather than just interrupting them. That philosophy has served me well so far.
